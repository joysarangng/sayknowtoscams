Job Scams

Scam Signs

  • Upfront payment requests before job starts
  • Being asked to open an account through unverified apps or websites
  • Receiving commissions initially then asked to top up more
  • Promise of high returns for minimal effort
  • Unsolicited job offers on social media or messaging apps, then moving conversation to a WhatsApp group
  • Lack of a specific job description
  • Communication limited to social media or messaging apps
  • Request for personal and bank details
  • Being asked to download unverified apps or set up accounts and pay in advance

Prevention Tips

  • Verify job offer authenticity through official company channels
  • Never allow others to use your bank account for transactions
  • Avoid downloading unverified apps from unknown sources for job applications
  • Never pay to secure a job offer


E-commerce Scams

Scam Signs

  • Deals too good to be true
  • Scammers offer items way below or above market price
  • Promotion of popular, sold-out items
  • Product information omitted, with hidden terms
  • May move conversation off platform to avoid detection
  • Request for direct bank transfer payments, offering discounts
  • Request for extra payments for admin and freight charges

Prevention Tips

  • Transact within reputable e-commerce platforms
  • Opt for cash-on-delivery or secure payment methods
  • Deal with reputable sellers with good ratings and reviews


Phishing Scams

Scam Signs

  • Received call, text, or email asking for personal information to claim a prize, secure online accounts, or investigate fraud
  • Fake websites created to mimic official sites of organizations or banks
  • Slightly different web address than the official site.
  • Inputting personal details, PINs, or OTPs on these fake sites risks information and money theft

Prevention Tips

  • Be cautious when receiving unexpected overseas calls
  • Never disclose personal, banking, or credit card details without verification
  • Do not share OTPs with anyone
  • Government agencies will not ask for personal details or bank transfers over the phone or through robocalls
  • Hang up if the caller cannot properly identify themselves
  • Verify information or requests through official websites, apps, emails, or company hotlines
  • Avoid clicking on URLs in unsolicited emails and text messages
  • Always type the official web address into your browser to ensure authenticity
  • Access accounts through the official company's website or app
  • Do not share sensitive and personal information without verification
  • If available, enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for online accounts
  • Turn on transaction alerts for bank accounts and credit cards


Investment Scams

Scam Signs

  • Message received from someone claiming to be from a financial institution on social media
  • Request for personal details like NRIC and passport numbers for an investment form
  • Asked to transfer money to banks in Hong Kong and China, or receive calls from people claiming to be from the Hong Kong Monetary Authority / Hong Kong Overseas Control Centre, asking for a deposit
  • Asked to pay administrative and security fees, and taxes to receive profits
  • Asked by online friend to invest in cryptocurrencies

Prevention Tips

  • Consult a licensed financial advisor before investing
  • Thoroughly research the company and its representatives
  • Use resources like the Financial Institution's Directory, Register of Representatives, and Investor Alert List on the MAS website
  • Dealing with unregulated entities can lead to limited recourse if issues arise
  • If an entity is based overseas, verify its regulation with the respective authority
  • Do not share personal and financial details with unknown individuals
  • Be cautious of strangers on social media promising wealth


Impersonation Scams

Scam Signs

  • Scam typically involves a caller claiming to be an official (e.g. local/overseas government official, bank staff, telco staff, courier company

Prevention Tips

  • Avoid answering calls from unknown overseas numbers
  • Never disclose personal details without verification, including passwords and OTPs
  • Banks and government officials will not ask for personal details or bank transfers over the phone
  • Hang up if the caller cannot properly identify themselves
  • Verify information or requests through official websites, apps, or hotlines
  • Avoid clicking on URLs in unsolicited emails or texts
